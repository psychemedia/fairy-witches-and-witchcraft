# Preface

A storynote collection relating to "fairy witches"; this is not a well-defined term, but applies well to two well known cases: the murder of Bridget Cleary, claimed to be a fairy changeling, but reported in the press as a witch; and the Roscommon witchcrft case, where a "witch" was supposedly in contact with a family's dead relatives who existed between two worlds (variously, this world and the world of the dead, and/or this world and the Other world).

To provide context, this storynote will also inlcude notes on fairy changelings, "white horses" used to travel between fairy forts, other flavours of "witch" as reported in the press.

Notes on "wise women" appear in other stroynotes (e.g. Ross-on-Wye stroynotes). Other storynotes that touch on the treatment of women include e.g. *wife selling* and **Skimmington rides*, currently in the the original stroynotes repo.


--Tony 'Monty' Hirst
